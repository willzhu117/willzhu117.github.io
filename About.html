History

Popeyes was originally named Chicken on the Run founded by Al Copeland in 1972. It ended up as a failed attempt at selling traditional southern fried chicken and relaunched as Popeyes Mighty Good Fried Chicken. This version found success using a new and spicy New Orleans-style recipe franchised its first location in 1976. The name Popeyes was a reference to the detective Popeye Doyle from the 1971 movie The French Connection.
